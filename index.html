What is Bullying?

Bullying is repeated and intentional aggressive behavior where there is an imbalance of power between the bully and the target. There are many forms of bullying — such as physical, verbal, social, and cyberbullying. Our website focuses on social bullying and cyberbullying.

Social Bullying

Social bullying is the intentional and repeated act of harming someone’s reputation or relationships, often by spreading rumors, excluding others, or manipulating friendships.

Symptoms of Social Bullying

Emotional and psychological distress such as anxiety or depression, behavioral changes like social withdrawal, and physical complaints such as frequent headaches or stomachaches.

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying happens when someone uses technology — such as social media or messaging apps — to intimidate, threaten, or embarrass another person.

How to Identify Cyberbullying and Social Bullying

Social bullying: Includes rolling your eyes or turning away from someone, excluding others from a group, getting others to mock someone, or damaging friendships.
